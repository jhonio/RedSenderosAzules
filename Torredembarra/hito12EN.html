The dunes


The dunes are one of the most characteristic habitats of the Natural Area locally known as "Els Muntanyans".



Crowning the dunes at the top there is a gramineae, the beachgrass (Ammophila arenaria). This plant looks great and slim, it survives in this environment so dry thanks to its long roots that go deep into the sand to find water. It is accompanied by a growing group of species quite interesting as prickly samphire (Echinophora spinosa) or sea holly (Eryngium maritimum). Both when the fruits are already ripe, separate from the roots to spread seeds rolling on the floor. Undoubtedly the most spectacular and threatened is the white sea lily (Pancratium maritimum), a plant that has the misfortune to flourish during the busiest weeks of the summer (and therefore easy victim of many visitors).
Inland after these mobile dunes or yellow, there are grey dunes, more stable. The pennyroyal (Teucrium polium) is small and grey, and the fleshy-leaf thymelaea (Thymelaea hirsuta) is a medium-large bush, both are the most characteristic examples of vegetation of the Natural Area.
If we pay attention, the traces on the sand will reveal the presence of many animals, from Montpellier snakes (Malpolon monspessulanus) and western spadefoot (Pelobates cultriples) to land urchins, through antlion (Myrmeleon formicanus). The larvae of these insects built cones on the sand at the bottom of which they await the arrival of any insect for staple it down and to feed them sucking fluids.
About beetles, you can see many of them, from the rare Scarites collapsing in the dunes during the day and coming out at night to hunt insects with their powerful jaws to the darkling beetles, a kind of beetles that are extremely diverse in desert habitats, being the most characteristic insects on these environments and serve as food for many birds and reptiles. Among the dune vegetation is possible to see the only pale coloured lizard that is in the countryside: the red-tailed lizard (Acanthodactylus erythrurus). This population at “Els Muntanyans” is the one that reached northern most in coastal global distribution.
