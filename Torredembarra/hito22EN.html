Birdwatching


During this trail walk you can enjoy for birdwatching, especially resting on the pond “El Saler”. We can not get much closer to the pond, so we could scare the birds that populate it, and also we should keep quiet. On the right you can see the vegetation behind the dunes. This is usually characterised by a high shorter than the gramineae high on the dunes or the reed bed between the ponds. It is very important not to go out of the path, as some bird nests are directly on the ground. Dogs are not allowed at this path because they harm the nests and, sometimes, destroy nests and eggs.



Leaving behind “Cal Valent” pines and following this path you can get inside “El Saler” pond, there is a small panel on the left explaining Flamingos life there. In this pond we can see the highest concentration and diversity of birds of the Natural Area. There are several species of water birds such as moorhens (Gallinula chloropus), coots (Fulica atra) and even, hopefully, flamingos (Phoenicopterus ruber). Unfortunately at this pond, food is given to ducks, what severely modifies the chemistry of the water and promotes proliferation of rats in the area. Bread is not the natural food of wild species, feeding them we do not help and also promote proliferation of rats. Avoid doing it!
At the crossing of wooden walkways. The one perpendicular to the sea connects the residential area of “Maritima Residencial Sur” with seashore and gives us a good vantage point of the pond. If we turn from the railway to the seashore we can recognise the different habitats in the space, one after the other: pond, rush, grey dunes, yellow dunes, and finally the beach. In all walkway crossings there are information panels of the Natural Area, there is also along the trail information some boards about the most common species.
If you continue straight on the path, now on a overpass wooden walkway, we find a interdunar channel that feeds the “El Saler” with seawater. If you continue walking along the walkway to “Creixell” municipally you will reach the tamarisk zone.
