Alfa-Omega


Offshore, and from almost any point on the coast of Torredembarra , you can realise the sculpture known as Alpha – Omega, the sculptor Rafael Bartolozzi built in 1999. This sculpture symbolises the end of no democratic councils and the beginning of democratic councils.



The sculpture is supported by the “Bloc”, an artificial island that was once used to support a system of pulleys to push the boats out to sea and return to sand. The other end of the cable was at the “Caseta de la Màquina” (the machine shed), demolished when they built the seafront promenade in 1994.
Promoted by the Council’s Department of Beaches and Environment in conjunction with the “Baix a mar Gussiaires” a local association and in order to preserve the historical heritage of the beach at the seaside district, in 2012 it began the process for returning traditional small boats called “gussi” to Torredembarra beach. The pass of time and the heavy work involved leaded to the disappearance of those small boats on the beach sand. The “gussi” was used for servicing a bigger boat, or as a fisher boat of humble fishermen. The presence of boats on the beach runs togheter with tourism and cultural offerings of the city. The house were is placed the driving machine for servicing the “gussis” is a replica of the old building where was placed the local sea rescue.
Backs to the sea, the trail continues through the streets of “Baix a mar”.
