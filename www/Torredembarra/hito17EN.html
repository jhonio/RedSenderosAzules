A natural beach is not an empty beach


A natural beach sand is not a desert. A natural beach is something "full". Full of remains of seaweed and shells, crabs, beetles and other insects, mushrooms, amphibians, reptiles, mammals, birds... And full also of a very special vegetation that must resist a very hard water shortages.



We can continue the Blue Trail by the seashore to “Clarà-Mar”. From here you can see seabirds, they usually nest during the summer in countries further north, especially in winter. The first and last hour of the day we can try to locate where gulls and terns rest on the sand, especially when there are storms. Please do not to disturb!
If terns would want to stop and rest on the beach during autumn, they will find that the beach is filled mainly of leaves of Mediterranean tapeweed. You could also see the remains of a much thinner tape and attached to a "stem" sometimes orange, these remains are from little Neptune grass (Cymodocea nodosa).
Walking by seashore, especially in winter, its a good time for watching birds like gulls and sea gulls (several species of the genus Larus), shearwaters (several species of the genus Puffinus), cormorants (several species of the genus Phalacrocorax sp.) and even terns (several species of the genus Sterna) and gannets (Sula bassana) or even a puffin (Fratercula) diving in seawater.
