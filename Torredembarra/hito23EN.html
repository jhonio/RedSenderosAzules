Torredembarra

Tamarisk area near “Creixell” municipality


If we continue walking along the wooden walkway, we reached some white buildings (called “Constelacion”), we are now at the municipality of Creixell . Along the way there is a small tamarisk forest (Tamarix gallica) . These trees are also specially adapted to brackish waters. They are able to capture the brackish water through their roots and extract the salt, which is concentrated in the leaves. An interesting strategy to survive in this environment so hard for vegetation.



At the end of the trail, there is the limit of the municipality of Torredembarra. At left hand side we will see a well established tamarisk area. Turn to the right path and head to the seashore. At the end of the path you will see that this has been buried by dune dynamics, it means due the wind. While walking you can see the reel bed, grey dunes, and the first line of yellow dunes.
Here ends the “Blue Trail” of Torredembarra. We can walk back by the seashore to “Cal Bofill” (where the trail begins), or return on our steps along the path next to the pond “El Saler”.
