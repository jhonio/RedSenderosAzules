The Lighthouse


This is located at the end of the Paseo de San Antón (San Antón´s Walk). Nearby there was an ancient chapel dedicated to San Antón. The present day lighthouse in Llanes dates from 1860. It sends out four flashes every 15 seconds.



It was begun in 1856 but when a huge swell brought down the construction they had to change and elevate the original site. The first light used olive oil; it was replaced by diesel oil which was used until the system was converted to electricity in 1920. In 1950 the light itself was changed. The tower you can see today is the original, having escaped damage in a fire in 1946. It is clad in stone up to the same height as the building and the rest of the tower is plastered and painted white.
