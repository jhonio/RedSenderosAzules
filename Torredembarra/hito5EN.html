Botigues de Clarà – Casetes de Pescadors (old fishermen stores)


Leave “Valls Ivern” square and continue the journey by “Isaac Peral” street where you'll find houses modernized but maintaining the structure of the old fishermen's huts.



Back in the corner we see the rear side of the old fish market, popularly called “El Pes”, which is currently a public library, and beside it there is the rear side of a two fishermen families shared house: “Cal Coco” and “Cal Joan de la Mercè”. We stop in front numbers 19 and 21, “Cal Brinca” and “Cal Roig”, two two-storey houses on white with windows and doors of different colors. Seeing them, we can imagine the stores some years ago when this quarter was a typical neighborhood of fishermen.
At the end of the street, you can see “Cal Bofill” gardens; we take the way to the beach and follow the trail, walking on the sand to the east, leaving behind the district of “Baix a mar” and the building of “Cal Bofill”.
