Isla Cristina


Situated on the western coast of the province of Huelva and in the southwest of the Andalusian region, Isla Cristina has a privileged position. Its origins are very recent, with its beginnings in the second half of the eighteenth century, when Mediterranean fishermen, mainly Catalonians and those from Levante, established themselves here to exploit the fishing grounds of tuna and sardines, salt the fish and extract salt. These activities still persist in current times and are an important source of income.



Following the earthquake of Lisbon in 1755, it became a definitive and stable settlement, under the name of Real Isla de la Higuerita or “Royal Island of the little Fig Tree”. Around 1834 this was modified to its current name, Isla Cristina in acknowledgement to Queen Maria Cristina for her humanitarian aid during the cholera epidemic. Its approximately twenty thousand people are noble yet friendly, with faces etched by the sea which warmly welcome the visitor and ensure that their stay is as enjoyable as possible.
