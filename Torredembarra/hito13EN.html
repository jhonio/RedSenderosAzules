Ponds of “Clarà” and “El Sol” by the path beside the railway


In the past, the area of the lakes like the rest of the saline was dried to transform the land into agriculture field (cotton, etc.). More recently it was used as garbage site for tons of soil and rubble, part of which comes from the railway, and still remains from that deposition a flat area used as parking near the road bridge of Clarà. The partial removal of these discharges uncovered ecosystems which disappeared mostly over the Catalan coast: brackish marshes that are now part of the Natural Interest Area of Muntanyans.



If instead of entering the Natural Area by the wooden walkway we get near the railroad we will find a path with the presence of Myoporum shrubs and tamarisk (Tamarix gallica) in the borders of the pond. We arrived at the bridge separating the lakes “El Sol” (on the right side looking at the sea) and “Clara” (on the left). This access connects the entrance of campsites “Relax-Sol” and “Clarà” to the beach area across all the different environments: marsh with reed (Phragmites australis, mainly) in the pond of “El Sol” and Salicornia fruticosa or glasswort vera (Arthrocnemum fruticosum) in the pond of “Clarà”, rushes (various species of Juncus), and dunes up to the sand beach.
We keep going by the path vhere we are, following the railway until you reach the underpass of Camping “La Noria”. At this point, you can return comfortably by the wooden walkway next to the dunes, or walking back by the sandy beach at the seashore, or by the path closer to the lakes. If there are still forces to walk, you can continue the Blue Trail walking along the beach towards Creixell, where we will meet more interesting sites of “Muntanyans”.
