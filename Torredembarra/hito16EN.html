Natural Interest Area of "Els Muntanyans"


At the underpass of Camping “La Noria”, you can continue walking along the walkway that leads to the beach area. By the middle of the journey, looking towards the sea, on the left, we see the most wild “Els Muntanyans”, hidden among the vegetation is “El Gaianet”, this lake is the smallest of the Natural Area and more protected, there is no path because the access is not allowed. At the back of the landscape, there are trees belonging to the residential area of “Clarà Mar”, about 700 meters away. Looking towards the starting point of the route, there are the typical ecosystem of coastal ponds.



At this point there is a panel explaining the wildlife living at the Natural Area of “Els Muntanyans”, also during the tour you can find more information panels on various topics related to area: weather, geology, habitats, lagoons and vegetation. In addition, during the journey you will also find small blue panels explaining about specific species (ten species are described: Flamenco, beach grass, reeds, glasswort, leggy, killifish, coot, kentish plover, and thymelaea sea lily).
Continue walking towards the sea until crossing a wooden walkway that leads back to the parking and to the lookout tower, along the way we can enjoy the flora and fauna specific of each surrounding (dune, marshes and wetlands).
There is also the option of walking back by the sandy beach at the seashore, or by the path closer to the lakes.
If you do not want to take the wooden walkway from the beach you can continue the Blue Trail and reach the residential area of “Clara Mar”, but we must do it along the beach, to avoid the space degradation (the high level of visits is the main problem in this area).
