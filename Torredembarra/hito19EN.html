Platja dels Muntanyans (beach of “Els Muntanyans”)


The beach of Muntanyans, is located between the beach and the township line of Creixell. It is part of the Natural Interest Area of “Els Muntanyans”. Suffice to say that the promenade ends at about 500 meters from the protected area. As general features it is a semi-wild beach with fine golden sand and with a excellent sanitary quality of bathing water. The seabed is nearly flat with some rocky areas. It is a 2200 meters long and an average width of 66 meters.
It is a remarkable place for its environmental importance, it preserves the last dunes and salty marshes in its 2.2 km length. Visiting is recommended throughout the year, as there can take sun and sea bathe, relax, enjoy hiking and observing even the species of animals and plants that live there. Of course respecting the environment.



From a historical and traditional view, beach of "Els Muntanyans” has always been considered a different beach by all the inhabitants of Torredembarra. This point of view is mainly thanks to coastal dune ecosystem, consisting of semi-mobil dunes and lakes of salt water with intermittent flooding and variable salinity, depending on the time of year. This is the fourth wetland in Catalonia after the marshes of the “Empordà”, “Delta de l’Ebre” and “Delta del Llobregat”. Its importance lies in the fact that it is a stop and rest between the Llobregat Delta and the Ebro Delta for birds that follow the migratory routes parallel to the coast.
This space, considered as a wetland, including the bath area of the beach, is a Natural Interest Area (PEIN) by appointment of Catalonian’s Government and is part of Site of Community Importance (SCI) defined by the European Directive 92/43/EEC. This SCI is called “Coasts of Tarragona” and the code is ES5140007. The SCI has been created to protected wildlife and habitats and because its natural and ecological value.
In relation to flora and fauna, is a wetland with many endemic species, both animal and vegetable. The area presents an important saltwort used as refuge for many species of birds and brackish lagoon areas, locally known as “Salats”, that satisfy migratory bird species needs. It is a biotope bridge for all the migratory birds. As animal species found: heron (Egretta garzetta), coots (Fulica atra), the mallard (Anas platyrhincos), Grebe (Tachybaptus ruficollis), Black-winged Stilt (Himantopus himantopus), Kentish Plover (Charadrius alexandrinus), lion ant (Myrmeleon fromicarius) and killifish (Lebias iberian).
As main plants found: marram grass (Ammophila arenaria), sea lilies (Pancratium maritimum), the plantain (Plantago sp.), Samphire (Crithmum maritimum), tamarisk (Amarix gallica), cattails (Typha latifolia) and various species of rushes and reeds (Phragmites australis).
The beach facilities on summer are:

  • 6 showers and 1 footbath
  • Service lifeguard in high season of bath
  • 1 module an 3 towers lifeguard
  • 6 wooden walkway and 1 concrete adapted walkway
  • 3 points of selective collection of waste
