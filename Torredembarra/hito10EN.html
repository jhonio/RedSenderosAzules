Ponds of “Clarà” and “El Sol” by wooden walkways


The Natural Interest Area of “Platja de Torredembarra i Creixell” or “Muntanyans”, can be visited by three ways: the beach, the wooden walkway, and beside the railway.



We started this journey in the gazebo near which there is located an informative panel about general issues such as climate, topography, flora and fauna in general and a schedule of guided tours that take place once a month at the Natural Area. Such panels can be found on the main access to “Muntanyans”.
In this case, we choose the path that runs along the wooden walkway. This path enters into the ponds, and it is a very convenient option for wandering and it is also accessible to people with disabilities. Its elevation above ground level, in addition to allowing better visibility of wetland allows the passage of a rare protected species of reptile, the red-tailed lizard (Acanthodactylus erythrurus), which otherwise their movements would be limited and in disadvantage against other more common species of lizards.
We arrived at the bridge separating the lakes “El Sol” ( at the left side backs to the sea) and “Clarà” ( on the right ) . This access connects the entrance of campsites “Relax-Sol” and “Clarà” to the beach area across all the different environments: marsh with reeds in the pond of “El Sol” and Salicornia at the pond of “Clarà”, and rush and dunes up to the sand beach.
Continue walking along the walkway and come to the section that connects the entrance of the camping La Noria, enjoying the scenery of dunes, marshes and reed beds. At this point, you can return on the same walkway along the dunes, or walking back to the sandy beach on the seashore or by the path parallel to the railway line, closer to the lakes to enjoy the flora and fauna. If there are still forces to walk, you can continue the Blue Trail walking along the beach towards Creixell, where we will meet more interesting sites of “Muntanyans”.
