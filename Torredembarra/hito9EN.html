Posidonia oceanica (Mediterranean tapeweed)


Walking along the seashore, on the beach, if you look at the sand beneath your feet, sometimes you can see various natural debris coming from the seawater. The most important are green or brown bands when they are dried. They are about an inch wide and almost a meter in length. They belong to the Mediterranean tapeweed (Posidonia oceanica).



This false algae (it really is a phanerogam, the phanerogam are flowering plants but algae do not -Mediterranean tapeweed blomms under water-) should be inside seawater, but unfortunately it is almost a desert. The Mediterranean tapeweed meadows should cover the seabed from the beach to 50 meters deep. One of its main role is to stabilise the sandy beaches, but also it has a very important function as it provides shelter for the young of many species of fish now nearly non-existent, also this plant increases the quality of sea water making it healthier, more transparent, and providing a livelihood to many animals that live in the sand, etc.
Mediterranean tapeweed meadows are considered underwater forests: one of the most well-structured plant communities. This is why they have a protected status at all law levels. This area is part of “Costes de Tarragona” area, which is a Natural Area of the Natura 2000 network. It takes approximately from “La Marquesa forest” at Tarragona municipally to “Els Muntanyans” at Torredembarra, it has a total of 1,110.7 ha and with nearly 85% of marine space.
Natura 2000 is a European network of natural areas that aims to balance the protection of species and natural and semi-natural-habitats with human activity. Keeping it from deterioration. In Catatonia, there are 115 spaces at the Natura 2000 network, them occupy a total of 1,062,371 ha. The terrestrial part is approximately 977,230 ha and the marine part is about 85,141 ha (December 31, 2012).
