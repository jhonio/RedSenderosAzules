The Perseids


The Perseids are a high intensity meteor shower. They may not be the biggest meteor shower, but they are certainly the Northern Hemisphere´s most popular and the most observed.



This phenomenon lasts quite some time and can be seen from 17th July until 24th August. Maximum splendour comes on 11th August with a Zenithal Hourly Rate (ZHR) 100, which makes it the third most visible shower of the year. This meteor shower is part of the Swift- Tuttle comet. Although the comet is not very near to planet Earth, its tail intersects our own orbit. Small fragments of dust from the comet enter the earth’s atmosphere at speeds of 212.000 km/h. At such velocity, even the smallest fragment of dust produces a brilliant stellar trail – a meteorite – on disintegrating. Given that the meteorites from the Swift – Tuttle comet come from the Perseus constellation, this particular meteor shower is known as the “Perseids”
